Jellied Eels, a delicious dinner for the ones that need it.

Jellied eels: The seafood equivalent of a wobbly adventure in a fishy jellybean factory! Slippery, slimy, and surprisingly satisfying, it's like a bouncy castle for your taste buds!

Ingredients you'll need for your shocking experience.
  • 900 g Skinned Eels
  • Malt Vinegar
  • 4 whole Cloves
  • 3 Bay Leaves
  • 8 Black Peppercorns
  • 4 teaspoons Salt
  • 1 Lemon (zest and juice)
  • 1 small bunch of Curly Parsley
  • Freshly ground White Pepper
  • Brown Bread and Butter

    • How to prepare.
    1. First, you need to prepare the eel. Using a sharp knife, cut the skin of the eel from the back of the head. Then, using fish pliers, pull away 2.5 cm of its skin.
    2. After skinning, lay the eel on the chopping board and cut off the head. Make a cut on the backbone, just above the lines of bones.
    3. Now, cut away the fillet. Keep the knife close to the bones
    4. When the knife is close to the bones, hold the eel from another side and cut the fillet in a clean sweep. Repeat the same for the other side.
    5. Then, take a large saucepan and add pieces with the ingredients, i.e., lemon juice, bay leaves, peppercorn, cloves, and salt.
    6. Pour cold water into the saucepan and bring this mixture to boil for about 20 minutes.
    7. Now, put the mixture in the bowl and cool it. After 10 minutes, stir it after adding parsley and chill it until the mixture is set.
    8. After the mixture is set and jelly is formed, it is ready to be served.
